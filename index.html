Why airport WiFi can be risky

Airports are busy, open environments. Many travelers connect quickly to free WiFi to check email, log into accounts, book rides, or manage travel plans. The problem is that public networks are shared by many people, and not every hotspot is trustworthy.

Fake hotspots

Some networks may look official but are not operated by the airport.

Shared networks

Other users may be connected to the same public network at the same time.

Login exposure

Signing in to email, banking, or work accounts on public WiFi deserves extra caution.

Simple steps to stay safer

  1. Use mobile data for sensitive tasks when possible.
  2. Avoid connecting to unknown or suspicious WiFi names.
  3. Turn off auto-join for public networks.
  4. Use a VPN before logging into important accounts.

How a VPN helps while traveling

A VPN creates an encrypted connection between your device and the internet. This can help protect your browsing activity on public WiFi and make your connection more private while you are away from home.

For travelers who often use airport, hotel, or cafe WiFi, a VPN can be a simple security habit.
